# Tall Text Mask Portrait Reveal

## Primary Use Case

A cinematic editorial reveal where the main subject image is embedded inside oversized condensed typography. The text itself becomes the frame for the asset, allowing the composition to feel graphic, poster-like, and premium without relying on separate image boxes or panels.

## Best Used For

- sports poster intros
- creator or founder hero reveals
- artist title cards
- key-character poster compositions
- premium landing hero sections
- cinematic quote or name reveals
- editorial campaign visuals
- bold identity-driven intros

## Visual Behavior

The central behavior is text masking: the asset is placed inside large tall typography so the letters themselves reveal the image. The result should feel like the subject is trapped inside the text form rather than simply placed behind it.

The reveal should not be aggressive or abrupt. It should enter with a blur-up and upward settle, giving it a more refined Apple-like softness while preserving the editorial poster power of the composition.

A secondary nameplate or subtitle can appear below the masked word after the main reveal has established itself.

## Motion Sequence

1. A subtle ambient glow establishes the frame.
2. The oversized masked word appears with blur reduction, opacity increase, and slight upward movement.
3. The text settles into place cleanly with no exaggerated bounce.
4. The lower supporting title or name appears after the main word is established.
5. A restrained ambient hold keeps the frame alive.

## Emotional Effect

This pattern communicates:

- authority
- identity
- boldness
- confidence
- editorial control
- premium clarity
- cinematic importance
- graphic intensity

The effect is strong, but the motion treatment keeps it elegant.

## Works Best With

- bold condensed fonts
- high-contrast portraits
- strong facial expressions
- sports subjects
- musicians
- founders
- creators
- dramatic side-lit imagery
- monochrome or limited-color grading
- one dominant accent color

## Avoid Using When

- the font is thin or decorative
- the asset lacks strong contrast
- the composition needs lots of small supporting text
- the message should feel soft or playful
- the asset crop does not align well with the letterforms
- the design needs a standard UI look instead of a poster/editorial feel

## Customizable Elements

- main masked word
- masked asset image source
- accent color
- subtitle or nameplate text
- blur intensity
- reveal speed
- vertical offset
- text size
- crop position inside text
- glow strength
- poster dimensions
