# Editorial Statue Panel Reveal

## Primary Use Case

A cinematic poster-style reveal built around two dominant elements: a large customized cursive/editorial headline and a bold red rectangular panel that establishes the composition before the main statue or character asset rises into place.

## Best Used For

- editorial poster intros
- monument or symbolic subject reveals
- premium hero visuals
- cinematic title-card compositions
- authority-driven static asset reveals
- magazine-inspired opener animations
- stylized historical or iconic subject presentations

## Visual Behavior

The composition is intentionally minimal. A bold red rectangular panel rises into the lower portion of the frame and acts as the primary compositional anchor. The main asset then enters from below with a slight curve-like motion, giving the reveal more elegance than a simple straight vertical slide. After the visual base has been established, the customized cursive headline reveals at the top as the dominant textual moment.

The final composition should feel sharp, editorial, restrained, and visually expensive.

## Motion Sequence

1. The red rectangle panel animates upward into the lower portion of the frame.
2. The main asset rises in from below with blur reduction, slight rotation, and a subtle lateral drift to create a curved entrance feel.
3. The asset settles with a small premium rebound.
4. The main cursive/editorial headline reveals from below.
5. The frame holds with very restrained ambient motion.

## Emotional Effect

This pattern communicates:

- authority

- premium restraint

## Works Best With

- statue assets
- portrait cutouts
- iconic silhouettes
- grayscale or desaturated subjects
- one strong accent color
- large high-contrast editorial headlines
- minimal supporting text
- poster-like compositions with clear hierarchy

## Avoid Using When

- the layout depends on many blocks of small supporting text
- the scene needs bright playful energy
- the asset is weakly cut out or visually noisy
- the composition needs multiple competing accent colors
- the text style is modern corporate sans instead of expressive editorial typography
- the intent is utility UI rather than cinematic visual framing

## Agent Notes

This animation should be retrieved when the request emphasizes a strong editorial headline plus a bold rectangular color field behind or beside the main asset. The defining trait is not just the subject reveal. It is the relationship between the red compositional block, the upward hero entrance, and the top-dominant expressive headline.

Distinguish this from:
- portrait eye-band reveals
- generic image slide-ins
- full-text magazine recreations
- UI card entrances
- typography-only intros

Use this when the user wants a poster-like composition with one dominant headline and one dominant accent block.
